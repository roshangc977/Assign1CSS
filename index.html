About the Song

"Stairway to Heaven" is one of the most iconic rock songs ever recorded. Released in 1971 on Led Zeppelin IV, the song is known for its gradual build-up—from a gentle acoustic introduction to a powerful electric climax. Its poetic lyrics and dynamic structure helped redefine what rock music could achieve artistically and commercially.

The song blends folk, hard rock, and progressive elements, creating a layered musical journey. Over the decades, it has remained one of the most requested and discussed songs in rock history.

About the Band

Led Zeppelin was an English rock band formed in London in 1968. The group consisted of Robert Plant (vocals), Jimmy Page (guitar), John Paul Jones (bass/keyboard), and John Bonham (drums).

Widely regarded as one of the greatest rock bands of all time, Led Zeppelin pioneered hard rock and heavy metal while also incorporating blues, folk, and psychedelic influences.
